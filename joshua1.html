❃ Joshua Chapter One ❃

Scripture

“Have I not commanded you? Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid; do not be discouraged, for the Lord your God will be with you wherever you go.” — Joshua 1:9 (NIV)

Observation

Joshua is stepping into Moses’ shoes — a huge responsibility. God speaks directly to him, reassuring him with promises:

  • “I will be with you.”
  • “Be strong and courageous.”(repeated 3 times in this chapter) God doesn’t just give Joshua a mission — He gives him presence. The success of his leadership isn’t tied to skill but to obedience and intimacy with God.

This is a call to lead with God, not without Him.

Application

  • Where in my life am I being called to rise up in courage?
  • What promises am I forgetting because fear is louder than faith?
  • God is calling me to immerse in His word, not just visit it. Am I taking time daily to let Scripture reshape my mind and ground me?

Courage is not the absence of fear, but the presence of God in the midst of it.

Prayer

Lord, thank You for reminding me that You go with me wherever I go. Sometimes I feel overwhelmed by what lies ahead — by responsibility, uncertainty, or even just the pressure to be strong. But You’re not asking me to do this alone. You’re asking me to trust You. Help me meditate on Your word, live in Your presence, and walk forward boldly. Make me strong and courageous — not in my strength, but in Yours. Amen.
