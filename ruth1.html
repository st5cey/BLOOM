❃ Ruth Chapter One ❃

Key Passage

“But Ruth replied, ‘Don’t ask me to leave you and turn back. Wherever you go, I will go… Your people will be my people, and your God will be my God.’” (Ruth 1:16)

Observation

  • There’s famine, displacement, grief, and loyalty.
  • Naomi loses everything and feels bitter (wants to be called Mara).
  • Ruth chooses faithfulness and leaves everything familiar.
  • God isn't speaking directly here, but He’s moving quietly behind the scenes.

Attributes of God / Promises / Principles

  • God is sovereign, working even through loss and hardship.
  • Though Naomi feels abandoned, we see restoration begin through Ruth.
  • Loyalty and love speak louder than obligation. Ruth had no requirement to stay — she chose love.
  • God can bring harvest after famine, both spiritually and literally (they arrive in Bethlehem at the barley harvest).
  • Bitterness doesn't mean you're forsaken — Naomi felt empty, but God had already begun the setup for restoration.

Reflect

  • Have you ever felt like Naomi — empty or bitter — even though you were once full?
  • Have you ever been Ruth — choosing love over comfort, walking into the unknown out of deep faith and care?
  • Where in your life right now are you being asked to hold on rather than walk away?

Application

  • Be a Ruth in someone's life — show up with love, consistency, and faithfulness, even when it’s uncomfortable.
  • Acknowledge your Naomi moments. It's okay to feel pain and be honest with God about it — but don’t stop the journey.
  • Recognize the timing of God: they arrived at harvest, symbolizing new provision and hope.

Prayer

Lord, I may not always understand your plan, but like Ruth, help me to choose loyalty, faith, and trust. In my bitterness, remind me of your goodness. Let me see the harvest ahead, even if I’m walking through famine now. Give me the courage to cling to what is right and the hope to know you’re working in silence. Amen.
